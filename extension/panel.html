  • ✂️ Be concise. Fewer tokens means less energy. Remove filler, greetings, and repeated context — get straight to the point.
  • 📦 Batch related questions. One prompt with three questions uses far less energy than three separate prompts, since the fixed infrastructure overhead is paid once.
  • 🎯 Match complexity to the task. Simple questions don't need reasoning-heavy phrasing. Avoiding words like analyse, step by step, or implement keeps the complexity multiplier at ×1.0 instead of ×1.5 — a 33% energy saving.
  • 🔁 Avoid unnecessary regenerations. Each "Try again" is a full new inference request. Refining your prompt before sending is cheaper than regenerating after.
  • ⚡ Use a lighter model when possible. Smaller models (e.g. GPT-4o mini vs GPT-4o) can use significantly less energy for straightforward tasks with comparable results.

The backend counts tokens using tiktoken.encoding_for_model(...).Frontend model keys are normalised (e.g. gpt-3.5 → gpt-3.5-turbo, chatgpt / gpt-4.1 / gpt- 4.5 / gpt-5 → gpt-40 ). If a model is unknown, it falls back to cl100k_base . If raw text is unavailable, the backend uses provided tokens_in / tokens_out

The complexity factor is determined by the presence of keywords in the prompt. Simple (quick questions, 1.0), Medium (summarisation, 1.2), Complex (reasoning/multi-step, 1.5). This multiplier scales estimated compute before energy, carbon, and water are derived.

Energy is estimated via piecewise-linear interpolation across three measured prompt-size buckets (400, 2,000, and 11,500 tokens) using model-specific Wh values from Jegham et al. (2025). This captures the sub-linear relationship between token count and energy, fixed infrastructure overhead dominates short prompts, while decoding cost dominates long ones.

Carbon and water coefficients are sourced per provider data centre (Microsoft Azure, AWS, DeepSeek) from Jegham et al. (2025) Table 1 by default. If Your Region mode is selected, regional grid intensity from Ember Climate (2024) and regional water intensity from Li et al. (2023) are used instead.

Where exact energy data for a model is unavailable, the GPT-4o class values are used as a fallback. This is a declared limitation of the estimation approach.

Sources: Jegham et al. (2025) arXiv:2505.09598; Ember Climate (2024); Li et al. (2023); Luccioni et al. (2024).
